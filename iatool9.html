Senderismo: A Short Story

The trail begins at dawn, when the mountains are quiet and the air feels untouched. I hike here always with the same ritual: slow steps, steady breath, and eyes fixed on the path unfolding ahead.

At first, every rock and tree seems the same, repeating itself like a rhythm, but with each turn, the landscape starts to shift in subtle ways I didn’t expect.

I take my time climbing higher, feeling how the effort stretches my legs and thoughts alike, as if the mountain is asking for patience before revealing anything.

The trail grows long, winding through shadows and light, and somewhere along the way, the noise of everyday life fades into silence.

By the summit, I realize the real change isn’t in the view below, but inside me: each hike reshapes how I move through the world, one step at a time.
