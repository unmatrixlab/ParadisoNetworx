Business Calculators

Use these interactive calculators to perform essential business calculations. Each tool is designed to be simple and intuitive, with clear explanations and formulas to help you understand the results.

1. Simple Interest

Explanation

Simple interest is calculated only on the initial amount (principal) borrowed or invested. It’s useful for short-term loans or investments, such as financing equipment. The formula is: Interest = Principal × Rate (%) × Time (years) / 100. Enter the values below to calculate.

2. Compound Interest

Explanation

Compound interest calculates interest on the initial principal plus accumulated interest, ideal for long-term savings or investments. The formula is: Amount = Principal × (1 + Rate/100/Compounding Frequency)^(Frequency × Years). Enter the values to see the total amount.

3. Return on Investment (ROI)

Explanation

ROI measures the percentage gain or loss on an investment relative to its cost, useful for evaluating projects like equipment purchases. The formula is: ROI = (Return - Cost) / Cost × 100. Input the cost and return to calculate.

4. Break-even Point

Explanation

The break-even point shows how many units you need to sell to cover all costs without profit or loss, perfect for pricing new products. The formula is: Units = Fixed Costs / (Price per Unit - Variable Cost per Unit). Enter the values below.

5. Gross Profit Margin

Explanation

Gross profit margin shows the percentage of revenue left after subtracting the cost of goods sold, helping assess pricing or cost efficiency. The formula is: Margin = (Revenue - COGS) / Revenue × 100. Input revenue and costs to calculate.

6. Markup

Explanation

Markup is the percentage added to the cost of a product to determine its selling price, commonly used in retail or manufacturing. The formula is: Markup = (Selling Price - Cost) / Cost × 100. Enter the cost and selling price.

7. Discount

Explanation

A discount reduces the original price by a percentage, useful for promotions or sales. The formula is: Final Price = Original Price × (1 - Discount/100). Enter the original price and discount percentage.

8. Loan Payment

Explanation

This calculates the monthly payment for a fixed-rate loan, useful for budgeting large loans like mortgages. The formula uses the monthly rate and number of months. Enter the loan amount, annual interest rate, and years.

9. Straight-line Depreciation

Explanation

Straight-line depreciation spreads an asset’s cost evenly over its useful life, used for accounting or tax purposes. The formula is: Annual Depreciation = (Cost - Salvage Value) / Useful Life. Enter the asset cost, salvage value, and useful life.

10. Current Ratio

Explanation

The current ratio measures your ability to pay short-term debts with liquid assets. A ratio above 1 indicates good financial health. The formula is: Ratio = Current Assets / Current Liabilities. Enter the asset and liability amounts.
